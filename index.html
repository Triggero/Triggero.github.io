Why HBO?

The moment I found out about professionally-oriented education and its differences from the standard academic approach, I realized I’d finally found my path. Why is that, you may ask? Maybe you aren’t asking, but I’ll tell you anyway. I found out I was completely inept at the standard, academic approach all the way back in middle school. It’s crucial for me to understand something I’m studying before applying it, otherwise I get lost in all the information. That became very apparent in math class. The teacher would present us with all these formulas and tell us which formula calculates what, but we were neither taught why it does so nor did we solve problems with the teacher, and that drived me nuts. I had no understanding of the topic and therefore I had no motivation to keep on studying it other than the obligation of me passing the school year which only made it a hassle. I still sucked at math and my substantial exam, the one before high school, was coming soon. My parents decided to assign me a private teacher. She would show me how everything worked, why it worked, gave me examples, and solved problems with me. For only a month, I made so much progress I was able to score a B on the exam, which was unimaginable for me. Moral of story: I still suck at math but I at least know the efficient way I can study. That’s why I chose an HBO, to work alongside the teachers, and not only know but also understand what I’m dealing with, which makes studying that much more than trying to remember and then recall blank information.
